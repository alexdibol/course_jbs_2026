§ 04

What Makes This Different

First Principle
Governance Is Not a Footnote

Every other AI course treats governance as an afterthought — a compliance box at the end. This course is architecturally built around it. Bounded inputs, schema enforcement, artifact bundles, audit trails, and fail-closed escalation are not discussed. They are implemented in running, inspectable code.

Second Principle
Finance Is Not a Use Case. It Is the Design.

Credit memos, investment committee artifacts, leverage covenants, board-reviewable inference, fiduciary boundaries — these are not decorative vocabulary. They shape the design of every notebook, every synthetic dataset, and every governance mechanism throughout the course.

Third Principle
Depth Without Compromise

Bayesian governance. Diffusion models. Goodhart dynamics in AI surrogates. Mechanistic interpretability. Multimodal lifecycle governance. Each treated seriously, with full technical rigour, and connected back to the central thesis: AI in finance must be defensible, not just functional.
