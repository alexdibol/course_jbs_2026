# AI for Financial Professionals (2026)

This repository hosts the landing page for **AI for Financial Professionals**, a 2026 course designed as the main entry point into a broader self-teaching formation in artificial intelligence for finance professionals.

It is the front door to the course ecosystem: a place where readers can understand the structure of the program, access the main course materials, and navigate toward the corresponding books, repositories, notebooks, and supporting resources.

## Purpose of this repository

This repository does not contain the entirety of the course in one place. Instead, it hosts the **landing page** that introduces the course and directs the user toward the full architecture of materials.

The course is designed as a structured learning system rather than as a single isolated document. The landing page helps the user begin in the right place, understand how the six volumes fit together, and navigate the wider body of content.

## About the course

**AI for Financial Professionals (2026)** is a multi-volume course built for readers who want a serious, practical, and institutionally relevant understanding of modern artificial intelligence.

The course is intended for financial professionals who need more than generic introductions. It is organized around the idea that AI in finance must be studied not only as a technical field, but also as an operational, strategic, and governance problem.

The overall orientation of the course emphasizes:

- conceptual clarity
- institutional relevance
- self-teaching readiness
- progression across multiple levels of difficulty
- practical access through repositories, books, and notebooks

## How to use this repository

This repository should be understood as the **entry layer** of the course.

A typical user journey is:

1. Visit the landing page
2. Read the overview of the course structure
3. Identify the relevant volume or pathway
4. Follow the links to the associated repositories and materials
5. Study the books and supporting resources
6. Move into notebooks and implementation where relevant

The landing page is therefore not just decorative. It is a navigation and orientation tool for the full course.

## Course structure

The course is organized into six major volumes:

### Volume I — AI Infrastructure
A study of the industrial and computational foundations of AI, including infrastructure, compute economics, and the strategic logic of modern intelligent systems.

### Volume II — AI & ML 101
A foundational layer covering machine learning and artificial intelligence concepts in a way that is explicit, pedagogical, and suitable for finance professionals building real understanding.

### Volume III — Prompt Engineering
A treatment of prompting as a disciplined and governable professional practice rather than an informal interaction style.

### Volume IV — AI Agents
An introduction to agentic systems, structured delegation, workflow decomposition, bounded autonomy, and human supervision.

### Volume V — AI Governance
A practical and conceptual framework for governance-first adoption of AI in professional and institutional settings.

### Volume VI — Modern AI
A broader overview of contemporary AI topics, frontier developments, current limitations, and likely future directions.

## Who this course is for

This material is designed for:

- finance professionals
- analysts and managers
- corporate finance practitioners
- investment and strategy professionals
- students seeking a rigorous self-teaching architecture
- readers who want both conceptual and practical depth

It is especially useful for people who want an organized path through the AI landscape instead of disconnected resources.

## Educational philosophy

The course is built around a simple idea: serious professional learning benefits from a layered architecture.

Different learners need different depths, different formats, and different entry points. For that reason, the broader course ecosystem combines long-form written material, repositories, linked resources, and implementation pathways.

The landing page exists to make that architecture usable. It gives the learner a map before asking the learner to navigate the territory.
